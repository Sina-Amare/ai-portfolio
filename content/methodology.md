# How Sina works

## Working style and approach
Sina is a hands-on, ownership-driven developer. He likes taking a feature from the problem
statement all the way to production — the API, the data model, the tests, and the parts of the UI
that matter. He learns mainly by building, and he is steady about shipping: meaningful commits,
tests as part of the work, and refactoring when it's warranted rather than leaving things half-done.

## What Sina is like to work with
A few traits come up consistently in how Sina works:
- **Relentless problem solver** — he doesn't just patch symptoms; he hunts the root cause. His
  employer at Dekamond put it as: "Never had to explain something twice. Never came back
  empty-handed. Your biggest strength? Being a problem solver."
- **Tool-agnostic and always learning** — he isn't attached to one stack. He integrates and
  orchestrates whatever fits, from coding agents and LLMs to traditional backend tooling.
- **Fast and creative** — he grasps problems quickly and is comfortable with unconventional
  solutions, which lets him move from prototype to production in days rather than weeks.
- **R&D mindset** — research, prototype, and validate before committing to an approach. He's
  comfortable in the explore-and-experiment phase, which suits LLM work where the ground shifts fast.
- **Patient and dedicated** — complex problems take time; he stays focused and iterates until it
  actually works.

## AI-augmented development
Sina treats modern AI tools as a serious part of his engineering workflow — as a reasoning partner,
not a replacement for engineering discipline. He regularly uses coding agents and assistants such as
Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, and Antigravity. His process is deliberate:
- **Problem analysis** — define the problem clearly, talk through the engineering aspects and edge
  cases, and research modern approaches before writing code.
- **PRD and architecture** — write a clear product-requirements document, choose the tech stack from
  the project's real needs, and design the system architecture and data flow up front.
- **MVP and iteration** — build incrementally with clear milestones, keep version control clean with
  meaningful commits, and test continuously while refactoring wisely.
- **Guardrails** — he favors simplicity over complexity, leans on root-cause analysis instead of
  surface bug-fixing, and stays current with new patterns.

His philosophy: AI is a reasoning companion that speeds up the work, but he stays in control, follows
solid engineering principles, and never blindly accepts a suggestion. The result he aims for is
production-ready code with a clear, maintainable architecture.

## Turnaround and availability
Sina is quick to respond and quick to deliver. He typically replies to messages within about a day,
and he's available for remote, hybrid, contract, or full-time work. He's based in Tehran (UTC+3:30)
and has prior remote experience, so working across time zones is familiar territory.
